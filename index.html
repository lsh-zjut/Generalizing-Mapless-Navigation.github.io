Abstract

Foundation models offer a novel navigation paradigm, enabling ``from understanding to action" unification in unknown dynamic environments via cross-task transfer, semantic abstraction and self-supervised learning. However, embedding foundation models into physically constrained navigation systems and realizing interpretable, generalizable control behavior in mapless scenarios remain an urgent challenge. This paper proposes a hierarchical modular navigation framework powered by a general navigation model. The framework consists of three core modules: (1) a vision-based waypoint sub-goal generation module for generating navigation waypoint sequences; (2) a plug-and-play local planning module that considers collision avoidance and smooth trajectory planning; (3) a control execution and recovery mechanism for achieving closed-loop robustness and adaptive navigation. The system does not require a global map or a localization module and features rapid deployment. Experiments conducted on IR-Sim, Gazebo, and real-world platforms demonstrate that the proposed approach yields a high success rate in explicit mapless navigation tasks and maintains stable obstacle avoidance performance in complex environments. This research showcases how general-purpose navigation models can integrate path planning, physical constraints, and control optimization into a unified hierarchical and scalable framework.
